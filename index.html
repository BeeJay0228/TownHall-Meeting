📋 Key Business Rules

▼
🔢

Claim Quantity Limit

Maximum 50 pending claims per AGG at any time. Completed agreements and auto-expired records do not count toward the limit.

⏱️

Claim Validity Period

Claims remain active for 5 days from initiation. After timeout:

  • System automatically cancels the claim.
  • Agent returns to claimable status.

Applies only to new claims created after the feature release. Existing records remain unchanged.

🔒

Exclusive Claim Rule

Once a claim is initiated:

  • Agent becomes locked.
  • No other AGG can claim that Agent.

Release occurs only when:

  • 5-day timeout expires OR
  • AGG manually cancels the claim.
🔄

Full Device Conversion

After Agent confirms the agreement:

  • All eligible POS devices under that Agent are automatically converted to New Policy.
  • Partial conversion is not possible.
📞

Obtain Contact Number

AGG can only obtain the Agent phone number from the Agreement Pending Page. The Agent must first be claimed before the phone number becomes visible.

✅ POS Pool Best Practices

▼
👀

Before Claiming

▼
  • Select the correct area. Both State and LGA are required. No data can be viewed without them.
  • Check your claim quota. Go to My Pending Claims and confirm pending claims are below 50.
  • Confirm the Agent is available. If already claimed, the Claim button will be disabled.
🚀

After Claiming

▼
  • Follow up immediately. You have only 5 days to complete the agreement process.
  • Contact the Agent promptly. Explain New Policy benefits.
  • Guide the Agent to: PalmPay Business App → Settings → New Policy Agreement
  • Monitor deadlines through My Pending Claims.
  • Cancel inactive claims early to free up quota.
📱

Contacting Agents

▼
  • Use the phone number available in My Pending Claims.
  • Clearly explain conversion benefits — higher commission opportunities and AGG support benefits.
  • Guide Agent through: Business App → Settings → New Policy Agreement
  • Follow up again after 2–3 days if there is no response.
✅

After Agreement Takes Effect

▼
  • Verify devices appear under POS MGT with New Policy label displayed.
  • Provide onboarding support to help Agent increase activity.
  • Monitor transaction performance via your AGG dashboard.
  • Use commission opportunities to motivate engagement.

Frequently Asked Questions

PalmPay New Policy is a profit-sharing model that replaces the old commission-based MMO system. Aggregators earn a percentage of transaction profits instead of fixed commissions, allowing for unlimited earnings potential.

You earn profit share from every transaction your agents perform. Card withdrawals earn 45% (cap ₦80-100), transfers earn 35% (cap ₦15-20), and Pay With Transfer earns 70% (cap ₦100). The more your agents transact, the more you earn.

Yes! When you purchase a POS under the new policy (Smart POS at ₦40,000 or MF960 at ₦32,000), you have full ownership. This means you can rebind the terminal, retrieve it if inactive, and it remains your asset.

You can claim agents through the POS Pool feature in your dashboard. Open the POS Pool, search by location, claim an unassigned agent, and contact them to activate the partnership.

POS Pool is a feature that allows Aggregators to claim Direct Sales Agents who are already equipped with PalmPay POS terminals. This provides zero-cost acquisition, instant POS ownership, and immediate profit-sharing eligibility.

TA (Transaction Analysis) Rate of 60% is a requirement that only affects old policy commissions. It does not apply to new policy profit sharing. Meeting the 60% TA threshold ensures your old policy commissions are not affected.

If an agent is interested in switching, you can move them to the new policy by rebinding their POS, reactivating them, and they start transacting under profit sharing. If not interested, you can unbind, retrieve, and reassign the POS to a new agent.

First check if the POS is faulty. If faulty, escalate to Service Center. If not, coach the agent. If they remain inactive, retrieve the POS and redeploy it to a new active agent to continue generating profit share.

Commissions are settled on a D+1 basis (next day settlement). You can view your commission balance, D+1 settling amounts, old policy commissions, and new policy profit share in the Commission Dashboard.

Self Check is a diagnostic feature on PalmPay POS terminals. Go to More > Self Check > Start Self Check. It tests Card Detection, Printing, Screen, Camera, and Network. Results show Normal or Issue Detected, with options to call support, visit a repair center, or submit an aftersales form.
